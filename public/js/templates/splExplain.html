Genetic testing for BRCA1 and BRCA2 mutations – Gene mutations are found in about 1 out of every 20 women with breast cancer. The genes are called “BRCA1 and BRCA2.” These mutations are also found in a small number of women who get ovarian cancer.

If we find these genes mutations before a woman develops cancer, we can recommend ways to lower her chances of getting breast an and / or ovarian cancer. Examples of these include lifestyle changes, medications and/or surgery.

For women who have already had these cancers before being tested, BRCA test results help them choose measures to reduce chances of cancer coming back.

If you haven’t been tested, this questionnaire can help you decide whether you might want to be. Please keep in mind that testing is expensive, imperfect and may have uncertain implications for your health. We recommend genetic counseling before having these or any other genetic tests.

Screening vs. diagnostic mammogram – a screeing mammogram is done as part of periodic health screening ... and for no other reason. A diagnostic mammograms is done to follow up a finding: typically either an abnormal screening mammogram or the evaluation of a breast lump.

Genetic female – a woman by birth, has never had transgender surgery, and in whom there is no reason to assume a genetic abnormality that would affect her gender.

If you HAVE had BRCA1 and BRCA2 testing and still have questions about what this means, please discuss with your care providers at your earliest convenience.
