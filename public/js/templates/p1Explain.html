Ovarian cancer – Cancer of the ovary is cancer arising from the ovary, the organ that produces eggs in females. The ovary has several types of cells. Cancer in the “epithelial” cells of the ovary, is associated with BRCA mutations. Up to 60% of women (6 in 10) who get epithelial ovarian cancer have a mutation in the BRCA genes.

Cancer of the fallopian tubes – Cancers in some organs near the ovary also more commonly have BRCA mutations, including the fallopian tubes (which carries eggs from the ovary to the uterus).

Cancer of the peritoneum (the lining of the abdominal cavity) – Cancers in some organs near the ovary also more commonly have BRCA mutations, including the peritoneum (a lining that covers the abdominal organs).

We group these three types of cancer (epithelial ovarian, primary peritoneal, and fallopian tube cancers) together when discussing “ovarian cancer” that is associated with BRCA mutations.

For more details see http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ovarian_cancer
