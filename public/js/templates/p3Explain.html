You may have wanted an explanation for:

Cancer – cancer cells divide and grow uncontrollably, forming tumors that can spread to nearby or more distant parts of the body. In contrast, benign growths do not spread throughout the body.

Breast cancer – cancer that starts in breast cells. There are several types of breast cells that may become cancerous.

Breast cancers are described by the type of cells involved and whether the cancer is confined or has spread.

When the cancer cells are confined (not spread), this is called in situ.

DCIS – One example of confined cancer is “DCIS,” which stands for ductal carcinoma in situ. This is a type of cancer confined to the breast milk ducts.
