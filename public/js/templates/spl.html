As you may know, mammograms are xrays done to look for changes in the breast, including changes which may suggest the presence of breast cancer.

This questionnaire gathers information that can help us identify and lower your chances of getting breast and / or ovarian cancer.

This questionnaire is meant for women who have not already had genetic testing for BRCA1 and BRCA2 mutations.

This questionnaire and its interpretation as presented here are based on widely-accepted, national guidelines for genetic counseling referrals (National Comprehensive Cancer Network, 2011 BR/OV-1). After you complete this questionnaire, a link to the NCCN web-site will be provided where you can go to find further detailed information.

If our interpretation of the answers you supply to this questionnaire suggests that your chance of having a detectable genetic abnormality is "Above Average" or "Indeterminate", you should not conclude that you will develop cancer. Similarly, if our interpretation suggests that your chance of such an abnormality is "Average" or "Indeterminate", you should not conclude that you are safe from developing cancer.
